About

Lewis Mason is a ceramic artist working primarily on the wheel, creating sculptural vessels that explore surface, texture and rhythm through making.

Recent work has focused on translating music into ceramic form. Through a synaesthetic approach, throwing becomes responsive to sound — where movement, tempo and repetition influence the structure and surface of each piece. This process has been developed through his Final Major Project, resulting in a body of work that balances function with abstraction and expression.

This work will be presented as part of an upcoming degree show exhibition, marking the culmination of his current practice and research.

Looking forward, Lewis aims to move into teaching — sharing knowledge of ceramics and wheel throwing in a way that reflects his own approach to making. There is a strong interest in developing workshops and educational environments that prioritise process, experimentation and engagement with material.

Long term, the goal is to establish a dedicated pottery studio space, offering throwing lessons and workshops to support others in learning the craft.
